Dog Shelter "Youumbba"

Don't Buy, Adopt

Interview:Jenny Kim
Photography:Youumbba

Jenny Kim is my old friend who volunteers in Youumbba Dog Shelter at Seoul, South Korea.
Jenny has been volunteering in Dog Shelter for 2 years now.(Youumbba Animal Shelter)
I chose to interview Jenny because I have a lot of interest in abandoned or rescued dogs.
I also went to the shelter twice when I went to Korea during summer vacation.
Below is the interview with Jenny.


Me: Hi Jenny, I contacted you because I was curious about the dog shelter where you volunteer.

Jenny: Hi Tina, I'm glad that you are interested about the dog shelter. I'll tell you as much as I can.

Me: I heard you've been working in a shelter for two years, right?

Jenny: Yeah, I've been volunteering at Youumbba shelter twice a month for two years.

Me: For those who are not friendly about the dog shelter, could you explain what the dog shelter is?

Jenny: The dog shelter is a space where it protects abandoned and rescued dogs.

Dogs in the shelter go back to their homes or they are adopted by new owners.

Me: What do you usually do there?

Jenny: I usually clean the inside of the cages, feed dogs, wash dogs, and sometimes I assist people who come to groom dogs.

Me: You do a lot more than I thought. Very surprising.

Jenny: Yes, once I go to the shelter, I work from 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. and the time passes so fast that I don't even know how it went.

Me: I'm sure so.

How many dogs are there in the dog shelter?


"More than 500 abandoned and rescued dogs"

An average of 260 dogs a day and 100,000 dogs a year are are abandoned in South Korea."

Jenny: There are about 500 dogs in the dog shelter where I work.(Number of Dogs in the Shelter)

Me: 500? I can't believe there are so many homeless dogs.

Do you know what stories the dogs in the dog shelter usually have?

Jenny: There are so many dogs coming to the shelters for so many different reasons.

Some are abandoned by their owners and rescued from the streets, while others are rescued from puppy mills or slaughterhouses.

Me: I think there are a lot of people who aren't really human.

Jenny: That's right. It's impossible to abandon a living life like that.


"Dogs that are not adopted get euthanized"

Me: How do the dogs at the shelter find new homes?

Jenny: Usually, dogs in shelters are put up for adoptions on sites like Instagram and Facebook.

Those who come to see the dogs will have to fill out an application and then follow some procedures before they can adopt.

We also send dogs not only in Korea but also abroad. Mixed dogs, which are not pure breeds, have many difficulties in being adopted in Korea.

This is because many Koreans prefer small and pretty puppies. Therefore, many dogs are adopted to foreign countries such as U.S. and Europe

where there are no preconceptions about the mixed breeds.


"Dogs adopted from Youumbba Shelter"

Me: Then what about dogs that are not adopted for a certain time?

Jenny: This is a very sad part, but dogs that have not been able to find their new homes for a month are unfortunately euthanized.

And this is because dozens of dogs come into the shelter every day and the shelter need to keep the numbers of dogs we can keep.

Me: What a sad reality. Of course, not all dogs will be adopted in a month.


"Donations run the Shelter!"

How does the dog shelter work?

It's a lot of money spent on dogs and nothing comes back to benefits for the shelter?

Jenny: The shelters run with donations. Many people donate a lot every month and many celebrities also make a lot of donations!

There are various ways of donating. You can donate money itself, and sometimes the price of purchasing our designed goods can also be donation.

The donation covers food prices, water prices, and electricity prices for dogs.

And there are many volunteers like me, so there are always people waiting to work for dogs.(Donation List)

"Calendar, postcards, and eco bag designed by Youumbba"

Me: There are also so many good people in the world! So my last question is, why are you volunteering at the dog shelter?

Jenny: I became interested in the dog shelter because of my dog.

Few years ago, when I was searching for dog supplies, I found out about the abandoned dogs.

I was very surprised that hundreds of dogs are abandoned per day.

Looking at the pictures of dogs posted at the dog shelter website, I was depressed to see so many dogs similar to my dog.

When I saw the shelters in poor environment due to lack of hands, I felt like I wanted to help.

And when I was searching the Internet, I found out a large-scale dog shelter called Youumbba.

Me: My question ends here. Do you have anything you want to say to those who want to volunteer?

Jenny: Not only Youumbba, almost every dog shelters are practically run by volunteers.

Therefore, I hope many volunteers who love dogs come and spend time with them and help create a pleasant environment for them.

Me: I hope so too! Thank you so much for allowing me to interview today.

Jenny: No problem! Thanks for being interested in the dog shelter. If you have any more questions, you can ask me anytime!
