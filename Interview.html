A Body of Knowledge: Interview

For the assignment, I interviewed my friend, Jenny Kim, who volunteers in Dog Shelter at Seoul, South Korea.

Jenny currently lives in South Korea and she has been volunteering in Dog Shelter for 2 years now.(Youumbba Animal Shelter)

I chose to interview Jenny because I have a lot of interest in abandoned or rescued dogs.

I also went to the shelter twice when I went to Korea during summer vacation. Below is the interview excerpt with Jenny.


Me: Hi Jenny, I contacted you because I was curious about the dog shelter where you volunteer.

Jenny: Hi Tina, I'm glad that you are interested about the dog shelter. I'll tell you as much as I can.

Me: I heard you've been working in a shelter for two years, right?

Jenny: Yeah, I've been doing it twice a month for two years.

Me: What do you usually do there?

Jenny: I usually clean the inside of the cages, feed dogs, wash dogs, and sometimes I assist people who come to groom dogs.

Me: You do a lot more than I thought. Very surprising.

Jenny: Yes, once I go to the shelter, I work from 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. and the time passes so fast that I don't even know how it went.

Me: I'm sure so. How many dogs are there in the dog shelter?

Jenny: There are about 500 dogs in the dog shelter where I work.(Number of Dogs in the Shelter)

Me: 500? I can't believe there are so many homeless dogs. Do you know what stories the dogs in the dog shelter usually have?

Jenny: There are so many dogs coming to the shelters for so many different reasons.

Some are abandoned by their owners and rescued from the streets, while others are rescued from puppy mills or slaughterhouses.

Me: I think there are a lot of people who aren't really human.

Jenny: That's right. It's impossible to abandon a living life like that.

Me: How do the dogs at the shelter find new homes?

Jenny: Usually, dogs in shelters are put up for adoptions on sites like Instagram and Facebook.

Those who come to see the dogs will have to fill out an application and then follow some procedures before they can adopt.

Me: Then what about dogs that are not adopted for a certain time?

Jenny: This is a very sad part, but dogs that have not been able to find their new homes for a month are unfortunately euthanized.

And this is because dozens of dogs come into the shelter every day and the shelter need to keep the numbers of dogs we can keep.

Me: What a sad reality. Of course, not all dogs will be adopted in a month.

So, the last question! How does the dog shelter work?

It's a lot of money spent on dogs and nothing comes back to benefits for the shelter?

Jenny: The shelters run with donations. Many people donate a lot every month and many celebrities also make a lot of donations!

The donation covers food prices, water prices, and electricity prices for dogs.

And there are many volunteers like me, so there are always people waiting to work for dogs.(Donation List)

Me: There are also so many good people in the world! My question ends here. Thank you so much for allowing me to interview today.

Jenny: No problem! Thanks for being interested in the dog shelter. If you have any more questions, you can ask me anytime!
